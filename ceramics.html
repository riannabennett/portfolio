Digital Design and Ceramic Casting


While completing my architecture degree, I have had the opportunity to work extensively with ceramic slip casting, with architectural and contemporary sculpture applications. I am fascinated with the current and potential use of ceramic components as architectural forms. My investigations focus on the advances in digital technology and fabrication and explores how this progress can be applied to ceramic architectural materials. I like to play with the interaction between historic craft, such a ceramic casting, and modern technology and theory.

Role: 3D Modeling, Lasercut Prototypes, Porcelain Casting

Intro

The plastic and formable nature of clay offers endless options for it’s uses in contemporary designs, achieving increasingly complex forms and ornament without sacrificing it’s structural capabilities. Prototypes were designed using Rhino 3D modeling software. A set of interlocking prototypes was built with polyurethane coated chipboard, laser cut for precision. The prototypes were used to create plaster molds to produce identical, slip cast, structural ceramic forms.

The Process

[ 1 ]
The process of creating a digitally designed, slip cast tile began in Rhino 3D modeling software. Once the design was finalized, prototypes were constructed using a laser cutter for precision.

[ 2 ]
2 part plaster molds of the chipboard prototypes were used to quickly produce identical tiles from handmade ceramic slip.

[ 3 ]
The cast tiles are finished by hard to correct any flaws that occurred during the casting process. They are then allowed to dry slowly to prevent cracking and warping.
