Pitch Deck


When I joined Dinner Lab, we were operating with a web app the had be developed quickly due to the company's quick growth. After closing our first round of funding, I was tasked with designing a completely new UI/UX that could accomodate our quickly increasing membership numbers and additional admin and user tools that were not supported on the site. Our platform had to support funcationality for members, chefs, employees, and partnerships like Google, Live Nation, and American Eexpress.

Role: Lead Product & Visual Designer

Intro

Dinner Lab invites members to try new recipes from rotating chefs across the country and give feedback. The dinners are held in unique spaces that exist for only 24 hours and feature chefs from some of the world's best restaurants. With over 70,000 members and X,000 chefs in 31 cities across the country, Dinner Lab is the nation’s largest supper club. Dinner Lab is more than your typical night out. It is an event experience company, with a logistical and technology backbone that has disrupted the industry, changing the way people think about eating out.

The Problem

After undergoing expansive geographic and membership growth, Dinner Lab needed to rethink the existing web platform to facilitate seamless experiences for event discovery and purchasing. Both user needs and new business initiatives were deeply studied to accommodate the best solution for all groups. business needs: private events, booking venues, chef booking, member tracking (catering to their lies / dislikes), event feedback, user needs: view upcoming events, browse past events, events in other cities, add profile information, add goes info chef needs: apply, upload menu, revise / edit menu, get feedback.
